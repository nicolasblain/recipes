## Ingredients

- 40 g (3 tbsp) softened butter
- 30 g (3 tbsp) all-purpose flour
- 140 g (5 oz) salted bacon, diced
- 1.1 kg (2.5 lb) beef chuck, cubed
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) olive oil
- 1 large onion, chopped
- 1 carrot, diced
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 45 ml (3 tbsp) brandy
- 500 ml (2 cups) red wine
- 500 ml (2 cups) low-sodium beef broth
- 1 fresh thyme branch
- 1 bay leaf
- 225 g (0.5 lb) white mushrooms, halved
- 170 g (6 oz) pearl onions, blanched and peeled
- 40 g (3 tbsp) butter (for garnish)
- 30 ml (2 tbsp) fresh parsley, chopped

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 165°C (325°F) with rack in center.
2. Combine softened butter and flour and set aside.
3. Blanch bacon 5 minutes in boiling water to desalt, drain and pat dry.
4. Brown half the beef at a time in oil in a pot, season and set aside, leaving 1 tbsp fat.
5. Sauté onion, carrot, and bacon in same pot. Add garlic, cook 1 minute, then add brandy until nearly evaporated.
6. Add wine (reduce by half), broth, and flour mixture while whisking. Bring to boil.
7. Return beef to pot with thyme and bay leaf. Cover and bake 1 hour.
8. Meanwhile, sauté mushrooms and pearl onions in butter until golden and season.
9. Add vegetables to meat, cover and cook 30 more minutes until tender.
10. Adjust seasoning and serve with pappardelle, potatoes, or toasted buttered bread. Garnish with parsley.

## Notes

Freezer-friendly recipe.
