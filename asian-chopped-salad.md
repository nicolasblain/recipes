## Ingredients

**For the Salad:**
- 3 cups white cabbage, thinly sliced
- 3 cups red cabbage, thinly sliced
- 1 medium red bell pepper, cut into thin strips
- 1 medium carrot, julienne cut
- 1 cup sugar snap peas, chopped
- 3 green onions, chopped
- 1 tablespoon cilantro, chopped
- 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
- 1/2 cup cashews, toasted and chopped
- 1/2 cup crispy chow mein noodles or wontons

**For the Asian Salad Dressing:**
- 4 tablespoons sesame oil
- 4 tablespoons rice vinegar
- 2 tablespoons honey
- 2 tablespoons lime juice
- 2 teaspoons fresh ginger, peeled and grated
- 1 teaspoon salt (to taste)
- 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper (to taste)

## Instructions

1. Toast the cashews in a preheated skillet over medium heat, stirring frequently until they turn golden. Remove to a plate and let cool completely before chopping into small pieces.
2. Wash and prepare all the vegetables. Arrange the white cabbage, red cabbage, bell pepper, carrot, sugar snap peas, and green onions in a large bowl.
3. Sprinkle the salad with cilantro, sesame seeds, crispy noodles, and chopped cashews.
4. Make the dressing by combining sesame oil, rice vinegar, honey, lime juice, ginger, salt, and pepper in a jar. Shake vigorously until well combined.
5. Just before serving, pour the dressing over the salad, toss well to combine, and adjust seasoning with additional salt and pepper if needed.

## Notes

A crunchy, vibrant salad with Asian flavors. The dressing can be made ahead and stored in the refrigerator.
