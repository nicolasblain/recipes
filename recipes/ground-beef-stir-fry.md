## Ingredients

- 400g lean ground beef
- 1 red bell pepper, sliced
- 200g mushrooms, sliced
- 1 medium onion, sliced
- 200g bok choy, roughly chopped
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated (or ½ tsp ground)
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp vegetable oil
- Salt and pepper

## Instructions

1. Heat vegetable oil in a wok or large pan over high heat until smoking.
2. Add the ground beef. Break it up and cook undisturbed for 2–3 minutes to get some browning. Then stir and continue cooking until no longer pink, about 5 minutes. Drain excess fat if needed. Season with salt and pepper. Remove from pan and set aside.
3. In the same pan over high heat, add the onion and bell pepper. Stir-fry for 2–3 minutes.
4. Add the mushrooms and bok choy. Stir-fry for another 2–3 minutes until the vegetables are just tender but still have bite.
5. Add the garlic and ginger, stir for 30 seconds.
6. Return the beef to the pan. Add soy sauce and sesame oil. Toss everything together for 1 minute.
7. Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve immediately.

## Notes

- High heat is essential for stir-fry. Don't crowd the pan — use the largest pan you have.
- No rice. The vegetable volume makes the portion filling without it.
- Swap bok choy for broccoli, snap peas, or cabbage depending on what you have.
- Add chili flakes or a drizzle of chili oil if you want heat.
