## Ingredients

- 6 tablespoons butter, divided
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 pound cremini mushrooms, sliced
- 1 large shallot, diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 1/2 cups arborio rice
- 1/2 cup dry white wine
- 4 cups chicken broth, heated
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
- 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese
- 2 tablespoons fresh chives, chopped

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
2. Melt 2 tablespoons butter with the olive oil in a large oven-safe Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add mushrooms and cook, stirring occasionally, until browned, about 8 minutes. Transfer mushrooms to a plate and set aside.
3. Add 2 tablespoons butter to the pot. Add shallot and cook until softened, about 2 minutes. Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds.
4. Add arborio rice and stir to coat in butter. Cook for 1 minute. Pour in white wine and stir until mostly absorbed.
5. Add the hot chicken broth, salt, pepper, and thyme. Stir to combine and bring to a simmer.
6. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and transfer to the preheated oven. Bake for 25 minutes.
7. Remove from oven. Stir in the reserved mushrooms, remaining 2 tablespoons butter, and Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese. The risotto should be creamy and slightly loose.
8. Serve immediately, garnished with fresh chives.

## Notes

- The beauty of this recipe is no constant stirring at the stove. The oven does the work.
- Use any mix of mushrooms you like: shiitake, oyster, or wild mushrooms all work well.
- If the risotto seems too thick after baking, stir in a splash of hot broth to loosen it.
- Dry vermouth can substitute for white wine.
