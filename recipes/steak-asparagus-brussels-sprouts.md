## Ingredients

- 2 ribeye or sirloin steaks (about 250g each)
- 300g Brussels sprouts, halved
- 1 bunch asparagus, woody ends snapped off
- 3 tbsp olive oil or butter
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- Salt and pepper

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 220°C (425°F). Toss halved Brussels sprouts with 2 tbsp olive oil, salt, and pepper, cut-side down on a baking sheet. Roast for 20–25 minutes without stirring — the flat side should caramelize and turn deep golden. Flip only in the last 5 minutes.
2. Take the steaks out of the fridge 20 minutes before cooking. Pat dry, season very generously with salt and pepper on both sides.
3. Heat a cast iron pan over the highest heat for 2 minutes until smoking. Add 1 tbsp oil or butter. Sear steaks 2–3 minutes per side for medium-rare (adjust to preference). Baste with butter and garlic in the last minute.
4. Rest steaks on a board for 5 minutes.
5. In the same pan over medium heat, add asparagus with a knob of butter. Sauté for 3–4 minutes until just tender with a bit of colour. Season with salt.
6. Slice the steak against the grain. Serve with Brussels sprouts and asparagus.

## Notes

- The key with Brussels sprouts is high heat and not moving them — that's how you get caramelization instead of steaming.
- Rest the steak. Cutting too early loses all the juices.
- Season the steak more than you think is necessary — thick cuts need it.
- This reheats poorly; eat it fresh.
