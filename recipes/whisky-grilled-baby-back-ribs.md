## Ingredients

### Ribs
- 2 racks baby back ribs (about 2 lbs each)
- 2 tablespoons brown sugar
- 1 tablespoon paprika
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon onion powder
- 1 teaspoon chili powder
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper

### Whisky BBQ Glaze
- 1 cup ketchup
- 1/2 cup whisky (bourbon works well)
- 1/4 cup brown sugar
- 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
- 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
- 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Instructions

1. Remove the membrane from the back of each rack of ribs by sliding a knife under it and pulling it off with a paper towel for grip.
2. Combine all the dry rub ingredients in a small bowl. Rub the mixture generously over both sides of each rack. Wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.
3. Prepare the whisky glaze: combine ketchup, whisky, brown sugar, vinegar, Worcestershire sauce, mustard, garlic, and smoked paprika in a saucepan. Bring to a simmer over medium heat, stirring frequently. Cook for 10-15 minutes until slightly thickened. Season with salt and pepper. Set aside.
4. Preheat grill for indirect cooking at 275-300°F. For a charcoal grill, bank the coals to one side. For gas, light only one burner.
5. Place the ribs bone-side down on the cool side of the grill. Close the lid and cook for 2.5 to 3 hours, maintaining temperature around 275°F.
6. During the last 30 minutes, brush the ribs generously with the whisky glaze every 10 minutes, building up layers of sticky glaze.
7. The ribs are done when the meat has pulled back from the bones about 1/4 inch and the internal temperature reaches 195-203°F.
8. Remove from grill, tent with foil, and let rest for 10 minutes. Slice between the bones and serve with extra glaze on the side.

## Notes

- The whisky flavor mellows during cooking, leaving a rich, smoky sweetness. Use a whisky you enjoy drinking.
- If you do not have a grill, bake the ribs in the oven at 275°F for 2.5-3 hours wrapped in foil, then unwrap, glaze, and broil for 3-5 minutes.
- For extra smoky flavor on a gas grill, add a foil pouch of wood chips (hickory or applewood) over the lit burner.
- Leftover glaze keeps refrigerated for up to 2 weeks and works great on chicken or burgers.
