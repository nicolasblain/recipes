## Ingredients

### Meat Sauce
- 1 lb sweet Italian sausage
- 3/4 lb lean ground beef
- 1/2 cup onion, diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 (28 oz) can crushed tomatoes
- 2 (6.5 oz) cans tomato sauce
- 2 (6 oz) cans tomato paste
- 1/2 cup water
- 2 tablespoons white sugar
- 4 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped, divided
- 1 1/2 teaspoons dried basil
- 1 1/2 teaspoons salt (divided, 1 tsp for sauce, 1/2 tsp for cheese mixture)
- 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
- 1/2 teaspoon fennel seeds
- 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper

### Cheese Mixture and Assembly
- 12 lasagna noodles
- 16 oz ricotta cheese
- 1 egg
- 3/4 lb mozzarella cheese, shredded (about 3 cups)
- 3/4 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese

## Instructions

1. In a large Dutch oven or heavy pot, cook the sausage and ground beef over medium-high heat, breaking it up as it browns. Drain the fat.
2. Add the onion and garlic to the meat and cook for 2 minutes until softened.
3. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, tomato paste, and water. Season with sugar, 2 tablespoons parsley, basil, 1 teaspoon salt, Italian seasoning, fennel seeds, and pepper.
4. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 1.5 hours, stirring occasionally.
5. Meanwhile, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package directions. Drain, rinse with cold water, and lay flat on wax paper to prevent sticking.
6. In a bowl, combine the ricotta, egg, remaining 2 tablespoons parsley, and 1/2 teaspoon salt. Mix well.
7. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
8. Spread 1.5 cups of meat sauce in the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish.
9. Layer 6 noodles over the sauce, slightly overlapping.
10. Spread half the ricotta mixture over the noodles. Top with one-third of the remaining mozzarella and one-third of the Parmesan.
11. Spoon 1.5 cups of sauce over the cheese.
12. Repeat with 6 more noodles, the remaining ricotta, another third of mozzarella and Parmesan, and 1.5 cups sauce.
13. Top with remaining sauce, mozzarella, and Parmesan.
14. Cover with aluminum foil (tenting so it does not touch the cheese).
15. Bake for 25 minutes covered, then remove foil and bake an additional 25 minutes until bubbly and golden brown on top.
16. Let rest for 15 minutes before cutting. This is essential for clean slices.

## Notes

- Simmering the meat sauce for the full 1.5 hours makes a major difference in flavor depth.
- The lasagna can be assembled a day ahead, covered, and refrigerated. Add 15 minutes to the baking time if going straight from the fridge to the oven.
- Use whole-milk ricotta for the creamiest cheese layer.
- This recipe freezes well -- wrap tightly in foil and freeze for up to 3 months.
- Serves 12 generously. This is a great make-ahead dish for feeding a crowd.
