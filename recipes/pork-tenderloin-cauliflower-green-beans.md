## Ingredients

- 1 pork tenderloin (about 500g)
- 1 small head cauliflower, cut into florets
- 200g green beans, trimmed
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tsp Dijon mustard
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp dried thyme or rosemary
- Salt and pepper

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 220°C (425°F). Toss cauliflower with 2 tbsp olive oil, garlic, salt, and pepper. Spread on a baking sheet and roast for 20–25 minutes, flipping once, until golden and caramelized at the edges.
2. Add green beans to the same tray for the last 10 minutes of roasting, tossed in a little olive oil and salt.
3. Meanwhile, pat the tenderloin dry. Season generously with salt, pepper, and thyme. Spread Dijon mustard over the outside.
4. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in an oven-safe pan over high heat. Sear the tenderloin on all sides until browned, about 2 minutes per side (8 minutes total).
5. Transfer the pan to the oven (or place on the vegetable tray) and roast for 12–15 minutes until internal temp reaches 63°C (145°F).
6. Rest for 5 minutes before slicing. Serve with the vegetables.

## Notes

- Tenderloin is very lean and dries out fast — don't overcook it. A thermometer is useful here.
- The Dijon crust adds a lot of flavour for minimal effort.
- Cauliflower roasted at high heat caramelizes and becomes nutty — nothing like steamed cauliflower. Don't skip that step.
