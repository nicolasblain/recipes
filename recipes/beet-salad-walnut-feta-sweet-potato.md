## Ingredients

### Roasted vegetables
- 6 medium beets, peeled and cut into wedges
- 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into 2cm cubes
- 4 tbsp olive oil
- Salt and pepper

### Pancetta
- 150g pancetta, sliced

### Salad
- 120g (4 cups) arugula or mixed greens
- 120g feta, crumbled
- 100g (1 cup) walnuts, roughly chopped
- ½ red onion, thinly sliced

### Dressing
- 6 tbsp olive oil
- 3 tbsp balsamic vinegar
- 2 tsp Dijon mustard
- 2 tsp honey
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Use two large baking sheets — beets and sweet potatoes should not touch or they'll steam instead of roast.
2. Toss beet wedges with 2 tbsp olive oil, salt, and pepper. Do the same separately for the sweet potato cubes. Spread on the baking sheets.
3. Roast for 35–45 minutes, flipping halfway, until tender and slightly caramelized at the edges. Sweet potatoes may finish a few minutes before the beets.
4. While the vegetables roast, toast the walnuts in a dry skillet over medium heat for 3–5 minutes, stirring often, until fragrant. Set aside to cool.
5. In the same skillet over medium-high heat, grill the pancetta slices until crispy and golden, about 2–3 minutes per side. Transfer to a paper towel to drain, then break into pieces.
6. Whisk together all dressing ingredients. Taste and adjust — add more vinegar if it needs brightness, more honey if the beets are earthy.
7. Let the roasted vegetables cool for 5–10 minutes before assembling (hot beets will wilt the greens).
8. Arrange greens on a large platter or individual bowls. Top with beets, sweet potato, red onion, walnuts, feta, and pancetta. Drizzle with dressing just before serving.

## Notes

- Beets can be roasted a day ahead and refrigerated — makes this much faster on weeknights.
- Swap arugula for spinach if you want something milder.
- Candied walnuts instead of plain take this up a notch for a dinner party.
- A few slices of avocado add creaminess if you want more substance.
- Goat cheese works as a substitute for feta.
