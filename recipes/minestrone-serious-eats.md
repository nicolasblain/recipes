## Ingredients

- 3 tablespoons olive oil, plus more for drizzling
- 4 ounces pancetta or bacon, diced (optional)
- 1 large onion, diced
- 3 stalks celery, diced
- 2 medium carrots, peeled and diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon tomato paste
- 1 (14-ounce) can diced tomatoes with juices
- 1 Parmesan rind (optional but recommended)
- 2 medium zucchini, diced
- 1 bunch kale or Swiss chard, stems removed, leaves chopped
- 1 (15-ounce) can cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 (15-ounce) can kidney beans, drained and rinsed
- 8 cups chicken or vegetable broth
- 1 cup small pasta (ditalini, elbow, or small shells)
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
- Freshly grated Parmesan cheese, for serving

## Instructions

1. Heat olive oil in a large Dutch oven or stockpot over medium heat. If using pancetta, cook until fat renders and pieces are crispy, about 5 minutes.
2. Add onion, celery, and carrots. Cook, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are softened, about 8-10 minutes.
3. Add garlic and tomato paste. Stir and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
4. Add diced tomatoes, broth, Parmesan rind (if using), oregano, and red pepper flakes. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes.
5. Add zucchini, kale, and both cans of beans. Simmer for another 10 minutes until vegetables are tender.
6. Add pasta and cook until al dente, about 8-10 minutes (or according to package directions).
7. Season generously with salt and pepper. Remove and discard Parmesan rind.
8. Ladle into bowls. Drizzle with olive oil and top with grated Parmesan.

## Notes

- The Parmesan rind adds tremendous umami depth to the broth. Save rinds in the freezer for soups like this.
- If making ahead, cook the pasta separately and add it to individual bowls when serving, as it will absorb liquid and get mushy.
- This soup gets better the next day. The flavors meld beautifully overnight.
- Make it vegetarian by omitting the pancetta and using vegetable broth. Add an extra drizzle of good olive oil for richness.
