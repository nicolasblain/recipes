## Ingredients

- 2 (14 oz) cans coconut milk
- 2 cups chicken broth
- 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast, thinly sliced
- 8 oz mushrooms (straw, oyster, or button), sliced
- 2 stalks lemongrass, cut into 2-inch pieces and lightly smashed
- 3-inch piece galangal (or fresh ginger), thinly sliced
- 4 kaffir lime leaves, torn (or zest of 1 lime)
- 2-3 Thai bird's eye chilies, lightly crushed (or 1 teaspoon chili paste)
- 3 tablespoons fish sauce
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 3 tablespoons fresh lime juice
- 1/2 cup fresh cilantro leaves
- 2 green onions, sliced
- 1 tablespoon chili oil (optional, for serving)

## Instructions

1. Pour the coconut milk and chicken broth into a large pot or Dutch oven. Bring to a gentle simmer over medium heat.
2. Add the lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaves, and Thai chilies. Simmer for 5 minutes to infuse the broth with aromatics.
3. Add the sliced chicken and mushrooms. Cook for 8-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through.
4. Stir in the fish sauce and sugar. Taste and adjust the seasoning -- the soup should be a balance of salty, sour, spicy, and creamy.
5. Remove from heat and stir in the lime juice. Do not boil after adding lime juice to preserve its brightness.
6. Ladle into bowls. Garnish with fresh cilantro, sliced green onions, and a drizzle of chili oil if desired.

## Notes

- Galangal has a distinct citrusy, piney flavor that ginger cannot fully replicate, but ginger works as a substitute in a pinch.
- The lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir lime leaves are aromatics -- they are not meant to be eaten. You can strain them out or leave them in and eat around them.
- For a richer soup, use all coconut milk and no broth.
- Add vegetables like baby corn, bell peppers, or bok choy if desired.
- Serve with jasmine rice on the side or spooned into the bowl.
- For a vegetarian version, substitute tofu for chicken and use soy sauce instead of fish sauce.
