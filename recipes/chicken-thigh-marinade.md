## Ingredients

- 6 garlic cloves, roughly chopped (about 2 tablespoons)
- 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
- 1 tablespoon honey
- 2 tablespoons low sodium soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
- 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
- 2 teaspoons ground cumin
- 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
- Juice and zest of 2 small limes (about 3 tablespoons juice and 1 teaspoon zest)
- 2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken thighs

## Instructions

1. Combine the chopped garlic, olive oil, honey, soy sauce, Worcestershire sauce, Dijon mustard, cumin, salt, cayenne, and lime juice and zest in a large bowl or ziptop bag to create the marinade.
2. Add the chicken thighs to the marinade and coat thoroughly. You can marinate at room temperature for 30 minutes or refrigerate for up to 12 hours. If refrigerated, let the chicken sit at room temperature for 15 minutes before cooking.
3. For grilling: Preheat your grill to medium-high (about 400 degrees Fahrenheit). Grill the chicken for 8-12 minutes, flipping halfway through cooking, until the internal temperature reaches 155-160 degrees Fahrenheit. Rest for 5-10 minutes before serving.
4. For baking: Preheat your oven to 425 degrees Fahrenheit. Arrange the chicken in a single layer on a baking sheet and bake for 15-18 minutes until the internal temperature reaches 155-160 degrees Fahrenheit. Rest for 5-10 minutes before serving.

## Notes

This marinade brings savory, slightly sweet, and spicy flavors to chicken thighs. Works well with both grilling and baking methods.
