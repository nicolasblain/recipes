## Ingredients

- 8 ounces linguine pasta
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 green bell pepper, diced
- 1 red bell pepper, diced
- 1 cup sliced mushrooms
- 2 green onions, sliced
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
- 2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning (or more to taste)
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
- 1/4 teaspoon dried basil
- 1/4 teaspoon dried thyme

## Instructions

1. Cook linguine according to package directions. Drain and set aside.
2. In a large skillet, melt butter with olive oil over medium-high heat. Add shrimp and cook until pink, about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove shrimp and set aside.
3. In the same skillet, sauté garlic, bell peppers, mushrooms, and green onions until tender, about 5 minutes.
4. Reduce heat to medium. Pour in heavy cream and stir in Parmesan cheese, Cajun seasoning, salt, pepper, basil, and thyme. Cook, stirring frequently, until sauce thickens slightly, about 3-4 minutes.
5. Return shrimp to the skillet. Add the cooked pasta and toss everything together until well coated.
6. Serve immediately with extra Parmesan and a sprinkle of sliced green onions.

## Notes

- Add sea scallops or crawfish tails along with the shrimp for a more decadent seafood pasta.
- Adjust Cajun seasoning to your heat preference. Start with less and add more.
- Half-and-half can substitute for heavy cream for a lighter sauce, though it will be thinner.
- Penne or fettuccine work just as well as linguine.
