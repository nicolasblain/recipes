## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon cornstarch
- 1 tablespoon cold water
- 1/2 cup white sugar
- 1/2 cup soy sauce
- 1/4 cup cider vinegar
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
- 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
- 12 skinless chicken thighs

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
2. In a small saucepan, combine cornstarch and cold water. Stir until smooth. Add sugar, soy sauce, cider vinegar, garlic, ginger, and pepper. Bring to a boil over medium heat, stirring frequently. Reduce heat and simmer until the sauce thickens, about 2 minutes. Set aside.
3. Place chicken thighs in a lightly greased 9x13-inch baking dish. Pour the teriyaki sauce over the chicken, turning pieces to coat.
4. Bake uncovered in the preheated oven for 30 minutes.
5. Turn the chicken pieces over and bake for another 30 minutes, or until chicken is cooked through and the sauce is caramelized.

## Notes

- Chicken drumsticks or bone-in thighs also work well; adjust cooking time as needed.
- For extra flavor, marinate the chicken in the sauce for 1-2 hours in the fridge before baking.
- Serve over steamed rice with the pan sauce spooned on top.
- Garnish with sesame seeds and sliced green onions.
- Make a lot of sauce
