## Ingredients

- 4 lbs boneless pork shoulder (pork butt), cut into 3-inch chunks
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 1 teaspoon black pepper
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1 large onion, quartered
- 4 cloves garlic, smashed
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 orange, juiced
- 1 lime, juiced
- 1 cup chicken broth
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

### For Serving
- Warm corn or flour tortillas
- Diced white onion
- Fresh cilantro, chopped
- Lime wedges
- Salsa verde or salsa roja
- Sliced radishes
- Guacamole (optional)

## Instructions

1. Season the pork chunks with salt, pepper, cumin, and oregano.
2. Place the pork in a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot. Add the onion, garlic, bay leaves, orange juice, lime juice, and chicken broth.
3. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce to a low simmer. Cover and cook for 3 to 3.5 hours, until the pork is very tender and shreds easily with a fork.
4. Remove the pork from the liquid and shred it using two forks. Discard the bay leaves and onion.
5. Skim the fat from the braising liquid and reserve about 1 cup of liquid.
6. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Spread the shredded pork in an even layer and let it crisp without stirring for 3-4 minutes until golden and crunchy on the bottom.
7. Flip sections of the meat and add a few tablespoons of the reserved braising liquid. Let it crisp again for another 2-3 minutes.
8. Serve immediately in warm tortillas with your preferred toppings.

## Notes

- The key to great carnitas is the final crisping step -- do not skip it. The contrast of crispy edges and tender meat is what makes this dish.
- You can also use the broiler to crisp the pork: spread on a sheet pan and broil for 3-5 minutes, watching carefully.
- Leftovers keep well in the fridge for up to 4 days and reheat beautifully in a skillet.
- For slow cooker: cook on low for 8-10 hours or high for 4-5 hours, then crisp in a skillet.
