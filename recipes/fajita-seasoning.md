## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon cornstarch
- 2 teaspoons chili powder
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- 1 teaspoon white sugar
- 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin

## Instructions

1. Combine all ingredients in a small bowl and whisk together until evenly blended.
2. Store in an airtight container or small jar.
3. To use: add the seasoning mix to 1-1.5 pounds of sliced chicken, beef, or vegetables along with 1/3 cup of water. Cook in a skillet over medium-high heat until the meat is cooked through and the sauce has thickened.

## Notes

- This makes about 2 tablespoons of seasoning, equivalent to one store-bought packet.
- Double or triple the recipe to keep on hand. Stored in an airtight container, it keeps for several months.
- Adjust cayenne pepper up or down depending on your heat preference.
- The cornstarch helps the seasoning mix create a sauce that clings to the meat and vegetables.
