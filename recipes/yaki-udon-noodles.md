## Ingredients

- 2 tbsp cooking oil (divided)
- 2 servings (500g) udon noodles (frozen)
- 1/2 lb shrimp (or chicken, beef, pork, bacon, tofu, or extra vegetables)
- 1/2 medium onion (sliced)
- 2 cloves garlic (minced)
- 1 cup cabbage (shredded)
- 1/4 cup carrot (julienned)
- 3 mushrooms (sliced)
- 2 green onions (chopped)
- Optional garnish: kewpie mayo, bonito flakes, toasted sesame seeds

**Sauce:**
- 1 tbsp dark soy sauce
- 2 tbsp oyster sauce
- 1 tbsp mirin
- 1 tbsp brown sugar
- 1/2 tsp gochugaru (Korean chili flakes, optional)

## Instructions

1. Season your chosen protein with salt and pepper. In a bowl, combine all sauce ingredients and set aside.
2. Soak the frozen udon noodles in hot water for about one minute to soften, use chopsticks to separate the strands, then rinse with cold water and drain completely.
3. Heat oil in a wok over medium heat. Cook the protein until it becomes opaque, about 2 minutes, then remove and set aside.
4. Add the remaining oil to the wok. Cook the sliced onion and minced garlic until fragrant, then add cabbage, carrot, and mushroom slices. Cook for 2-3 minutes until vegetables soften slightly.
5. Return the cooked protein to the wok. Add the prepared noodles and pour in the sauce mixture. Toss everything together until evenly coated. Finish with chopped green onions and any optional garnishes you prefer.

## Notes

A quick and flavorful stir-fried noodle dish that comes together in just 15 minutes. Versatile protein options make it adaptable to dietary preferences.
