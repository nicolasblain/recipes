## Ingredients

- 4 filet mignon steaks (6 oz each), pounded to 1/2 inch thickness
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, divided
- 2 shallots, finely diced
- 8 oz button mushrooms, finely sliced
- 2 cloves garlic, finely minced
- 1/4 cup brandy
- 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
- 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
- 3/4 cup heavy cream
- 2 tablespoons flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- 1 teaspoon fresh lemon juice

## Instructions

1. Season the steaks generously with salt and pepper on both sides.
2. Heat the olive oil and 1 tablespoon butter in a large heavy skillet over very high heat until smoking hot.
3. Lay the steaks away from you into the pan. Sear for 2 minutes on each side for medium-rare, getting a deep golden-brown crust. Remove and rest on a warm plate.
4. Lower the heat to medium. Add 1 tablespoon butter to the pan. Add the shallots and cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly.
5. Add the mushrooms, season with a pinch of salt, and cook for 3-4 minutes until golden and all the moisture has evaporated.
6. Add the garlic and cook for 30 seconds.
7. Pull the pan off the heat and add the brandy. Return to the heat and allow the alcohol to cook off completely, about 1 minute (or carefully flambe).
8. Stir in the Worcestershire sauce and Dijon mustard, mixing thoroughly into the mushrooms.
9. Pour in the cream and bring to a gentle simmer. Let it reduce for 2-3 minutes until the sauce coats the back of a spoon.
10. Finish the sauce with the remaining tablespoon of butter and the lemon juice. Taste and adjust seasoning.
11. Return the steaks to the pan along with any resting juices. Baste with the sauce for 30 seconds.
12. Plate the steaks, spoon the sauce generously over the top, and finish with chopped parsley.

## Notes

- Ramsay's version emphasizes getting the pan extremely hot for a proper sear -- do not overcrowd the pan.
- Pounding the steaks thin ensures quick, even cooking, which is characteristic of the dish.
- The Worcestershire sauce is more prominent in this version, giving it a deeper savory flavor.
- Serve with buttery mashed potatoes or sauteed green beans.
- The sauce can be made ahead and reheated; add a splash of cream when rewarming.
