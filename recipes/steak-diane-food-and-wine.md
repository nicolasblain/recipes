## Ingredients

- 4 filet mignon steaks (6 oz each), about 1 inch thick
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, divided
- 1/2 lb cremini or button mushrooms, thinly sliced
- 2 shallots, finely minced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/4 cup cognac or brandy
- 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
- 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
- 1/2 cup heavy cream
- 2 tablespoons fresh chives, snipped
- 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice

## Instructions

1. Pat the steaks dry and season generously with salt and pepper. If desired, pound them slightly to an even 3/4-inch thickness.
2. Heat the oil and 1 tablespoon of butter in a large skillet over high heat until the butter stops foaming.
3. Sear the steaks for 3-4 minutes per side for medium-rare. Transfer to a plate and tent loosely with foil.
4. Reduce heat to medium. Add 1 tablespoon of butter to the same skillet. Add the mushrooms and cook until golden, about 4-5 minutes.
5. Add the shallots and garlic, cooking for 1 minute until fragrant.
6. Remove the pan from heat and carefully add the cognac. Return to heat and ignite with a long match or let the alcohol cook off for 1-2 minutes.
7. Stir in the Dijon mustard and Worcestershire sauce.
8. Add the heavy cream and bring to a simmer. Cook for 2-3 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.
9. Swirl in the remaining 1 tablespoon of butter and add the lemon juice. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
10. Return the steaks and any accumulated juices to the pan. Spoon sauce over the steaks and cook for 1 minute to warm through.
11. Serve immediately, topped with the sauce and garnished with chives.

## Notes

- Steak Diane is a classic tableside dish from the mid-20th century steakhouse tradition.
- For a dramatic presentation, flambe the cognac at the table (use caution and keep a lid nearby).
- The key to a great Diane sauce is the balance of mustard, Worcestershire, and cream.
- Serve with mashed potatoes or crispy frites to soak up the sauce.
