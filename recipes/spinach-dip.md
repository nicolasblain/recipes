## Ingredients

- 1 (10 oz) package frozen chopped spinach, thawed and well drained
- 1 cup sour cream
- 1 cup mayonnaise
- 1 (1 oz) package dry vegetable soup mix (such as Knorr)
- 1 (8 oz) can water chestnuts, drained and chopped
- 3 green onions, chopped
- Salt and pepper to taste

### For Serving
- 1 round loaf of bread (pumpernickel, sourdough, or Hawaiian)
- Crackers, vegetables, or bread cubes for dipping

## Instructions

1. Squeeze the thawed spinach in a clean kitchen towel or paper towels to remove as much moisture as possible. This is the most important step for a thick, creamy dip.
2. In a large bowl, combine the sour cream, mayonnaise, and dry vegetable soup mix. Stir until well blended.
3. Add the drained spinach, chopped water chestnuts, and green onions. Stir to combine evenly.
4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
5. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours (overnight is best) to allow the flavors to meld.
6. To serve in a bread bowl, cut the top off the bread loaf and hollow out the center. Cut the removed bread into cubes for dipping.
7. Spoon the chilled dip into the bread bowl and serve with bread cubes, crackers, or cut vegetables.

## Notes

- Draining the spinach thoroughly is essential. Excess water will make the dip runny.
- This dip tastes best when made a day ahead, as the soup mix needs time to fully hydrate and develop flavor.
- For a lighter version, substitute Greek yogurt for some or all of the sour cream.
- Keeps well refrigerated for up to 3 days.
