## Ingredients

- 1 lb. dried elbow pasta
- 1/2 cup unsalted butter
- 1/2 cup all purpose flour
- 1 1/2 cups whole milk
- 2 1/2 cups half and half
- 4 cups shredded medium cheddar cheese (divided)
- 2 cups shredded Gruyere cheese (divided)
- 1/2 Tbsp. salt
- 1/2 tsp. black pepper
- 1/4 tsp. paprika

## Instructions

1. Preheat your oven to 325 degrees Fahrenheit and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
2. Boil a large pot of salted water, add the pasta, and cook for 1 minute less than the package directions suggest. Drain the pasta and toss with a small amount of olive oil to prevent sticking.
3. While the water is heating, shred the cheeses and divide them into three portions: about 3 cups for the sauce, 1.5 cups for the middle layer, and 1.5 cups for the top.
4. Melt the butter in a large saucepan over medium heat. Whisk in the flour to create a paste, then gradually whisk in the half and half and milk until smooth and well combined.
5. Heat the mixture over medium heat, whisking frequently, until it thickens to a consistency similar to condensed soup.
6. Remove the pan from the heat and stir in the salt, pepper, paprika, and 3 cups of cheese until the cheese melts and the sauce is smooth.
7. Combine the drained pasta with the cheese sauce. Pour half of this mixture into the prepared baking dish, top with 1.5 cups of cheese, then add the remaining pasta mixture on top.
8. Sprinkle the final 1.5 cups of cheese over the top and bake for 15 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.

## Notes

A comforting, creamy baked mac and cheese that feeds a crowd. The combination of cheddar and Gruyere creates rich flavor.
