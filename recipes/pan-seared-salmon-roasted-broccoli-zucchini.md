## Ingredients

- 2 salmon fillets (about 180g each), skin-on
- 1 head broccoli, cut into florets
- 2 medium zucchini, cut into half-moons
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- Salt and pepper
- Lemon wedges to serve

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 220°C (425°F). Toss the broccoli and zucchini with 2 tbsp olive oil, garlic, salt, and pepper. Spread on a baking sheet in a single layer — don't crowd or they'll steam instead of roast.
2. Roast for 20–25 minutes, flipping once halfway, until edges are slightly charred and crispy. This is what makes the vegetables satisfying.
3. About 10 minutes before the vegetables are done, cook the salmon. Pat fillets dry with paper towel and season well with salt and pepper.
4. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a pan over high heat until almost smoking. Place salmon skin-side down. Press gently with a spatula for the first 30 seconds to prevent curling.
5. Cook skin-side down for 4–5 minutes without touching, until skin is crispy and the flesh is cooked about 75% through (you'll see it turning from translucent to opaque up the sides).
6. Flip and cook for 1–2 more minutes. Remove from heat.
7. Serve salmon over the roasted vegetables with lemon wedges.

## Notes

- Dry the salmon — moisture is the enemy of a good sear. If the fillets are wet, the skin won't crisp.
- High oven heat for the vegetables is non-negotiable. Lower temps produce limp, watery veg.
- Don't move the salmon while the skin is cooking or it will tear.
