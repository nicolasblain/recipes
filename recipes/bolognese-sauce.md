## Ingredients

### Sauce
- 2 tbsp (30 ml) olive oil
- 115g (4 oz) pancetta, diced
- 1 large onion, finely diced
- 2 carrots, finely diced
- 2 stalks celery, finely diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 450g (1 lb) ground veal
- 450g (1 lb) ground pork
- 1 cup (250 ml) dry white wine
- 3 tbsp (45 ml) tomato paste
- 2 cups (500 ml) beef broth
- 1 cup (250 ml) whole milk
- Salt and pepper

### To serve
- 675g (1½ lb) fresh tagliatelle or pappardelle
- 2 tbsp (30 ml) butter
- Freshly grated Parmesan

## Instructions

1. In a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the pancetta and cook for 3–4 minutes until it renders its fat and starts to crisp. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside.
2. In the same pot, add the onion, carrots, and celery. Cook over medium-low heat for 8–10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until very soft and just starting to colour. Add the garlic and cook for 1 more minute.
3. Increase heat to medium-high. Add the ground veal and pork. Break up the meat well and cook until no longer pink, about 8 minutes. Season generously with salt and pepper.
4. Return the pancetta to the pot. Add the white wine and scrape up any browned bits from the bottom. Let the wine reduce almost completely, about 5 minutes.
5. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes, stirring constantly.
6. Add the broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce to the lowest possible simmer. Cook uncovered for 2 to 2½ hours, stirring occasionally, until the sauce is thick and the fat has risen to the surface. Add a splash of broth or water if it gets too dry.
7. Stir in the milk and simmer for another 20 minutes. Taste and adjust seasoning.
8. Cook the pasta in well-salted boiling water. Drain, reserving ½ cup of pasta water, and toss with butter. Add the sauce and a splash of pasta water to loosen if needed. Finish with Parmesan.

## Notes

- The key to this recipe is patience — the long simmer is what makes it. Don't rush step 6.
- Tomato paste only, no crushed tomatoes. The bolognese should be meaty, not tomatoey.
- White wine is traditional for authentic bolognese. Red works but changes the flavour profile noticeably.
- The sauce freezes very well — make the full batch and freeze in portions.
- Leftovers are even better the next day.
