## Ingredients

**For the Sour Cream:**
- Oil for cooking
- 1/2 cup diced yellow onion
- 1 tbsp minced jalapeño pepper
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 1 cup sour cream

**For the Tacos:**
- Olive oil for cooking
- 2 cups sweet potato, cubed
- 1/2 cup red bell pepper, cubed
- Salt and pepper
- 1 cup canned black beans (rinsed, drained)
- 1 cup frozen corn kernels
- 1 tsp chili seasoning
- 1/2 cup chili sauce
- 1/4 cup water
- Juice of 1/2 lime
- 1/4 cup fresh cilantro
- 1 cup shredded monterey jack cheese
- 10-12 soft taco shells

## Instructions

1. Prepare the onion sour cream by sautéing the diced onion and minced jalapeño in oil until they are well browned. Season with salt and pepper, then stir into the sour cream. Refrigerate until ready to serve.
2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the cubed sweet potato and red bell pepper, and cook for 5-6 minutes until beginning to soften. Season with salt and pepper.
3. Add the rinsed black beans, frozen corn, chili seasoning, chili sauce, and water to the skillet. Simmer together for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
4. Stir in the lime juice and fresh cilantro to finish the filling.
5. Transfer the taco filling to a baking dish and top with the shredded monterey jack cheese.
6. Broil in the oven until the cheese is melted and lightly browned.
7. Warm the taco shells and serve the filling with the onion sour cream on the side.

## Notes

A flavorful vegetarian taco filling with roasted vegetables and beans, topped with melted cheese. The caramelized onion sour cream adds rich flavor.
