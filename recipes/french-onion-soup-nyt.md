## Ingredients

- 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 3 pounds yellow onions (about 6 large), thinly sliced
- 1 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
- 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
- 1 cup dry white wine or dry vermouth
- 6 cups beef broth
- 2 cups chicken broth
- 2 tablespoons cognac or brandy (optional)
- 1 bay leaf
- 2 sprigs fresh thyme (or 1/2 teaspoon dried)
- 6-8 slices French bread, about 3/4 inch thick, toasted
- 2 cups grated Gruyere cheese (about 8 ounces)
- 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese

## Instructions

1. Melt butter in a large heavy-bottomed Dutch oven or stockpot over medium heat. Add all the sliced onions, salt, and pepper. Stir to coat with butter.
2. Cook onions, stirring occasionally, for about 15 minutes until softened and beginning to turn golden.
3. Reduce heat to medium-low and sprinkle sugar over the onions. Continue to cook, stirring every few minutes, for 45 minutes to 1 hour, until the onions are deeply caramelized and mahogany brown. Do not rush this step.
4. Sprinkle flour over the onions and stir for 2 minutes to cook out the raw flour taste.
5. Add the wine and stir, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot. Let the wine reduce for 2 minutes.
6. Add the beef broth, chicken broth, bay leaf, and thyme. Bring to a simmer and cook for 20-30 minutes. Add cognac if using. Taste and adjust seasoning. Discard bay leaf and thyme sprigs.
7. Preheat the broiler. Ladle soup into oven-safe crocks or bowls. Place on a baking sheet.
8. Top each bowl with 1-2 slices of toasted bread. Generously cover with Gruyere and sprinkle with Parmesan.
9. Broil until the cheese is melted, bubbly, and browned in spots, about 3-5 minutes. Watch carefully to prevent burning.
10. Serve immediately.

## Notes

- Low and slow caramelization of the onions is the key to this soup. Resist the urge to turn up the heat.
- A combination of beef and chicken broth gives the best depth of flavor.
- If you do not have oven-safe bowls, melt the cheese on the toasted bread on a sheet pan under the broiler, then float the cheesy toasts on the soup.
- The soup base (without bread and cheese) freezes well for up to 3 months.
