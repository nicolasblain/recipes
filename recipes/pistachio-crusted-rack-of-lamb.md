## Ingredients

- 2 racks of lamb (about 1.5 lbs each), frenched
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 3 tablespoons Dijon mustard
- 1 cup shelled pistachios, finely chopped
- 1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs
- 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, finely chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons butter, melted
- 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
2. Season the racks of lamb generously with salt and pepper.
3. Heat olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet over high heat. Sear the lamb racks fat-side down for 2 minutes, then sear on all sides until browned, about 1-2 minutes per side. Remove from heat and let rest for 5 minutes.
4. In a bowl, combine the chopped pistachios, panko breadcrumbs, parsley, garlic, melted butter, and thyme. Mix until evenly combined.
5. Brush the top and sides of each rack of lamb generously with Dijon mustard.
6. Press the pistachio mixture firmly onto the mustard-coated surfaces, forming an even crust.
7. Place the racks in a roasting pan or back in the skillet, bone-side down.
8. Roast in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes for medium-rare (internal temperature of 130°F) or 25-30 minutes for medium (140°F).
9. Remove from oven and tent loosely with foil. Let rest for 10 minutes before slicing between the bones to serve.

## Notes

- Ask your butcher to french the racks (clean the bones) for a nicer presentation.
- Bring the lamb to room temperature for 30 minutes before cooking for more even results.
- The pistachio crust can be prepared ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator.
- A meat thermometer is strongly recommended to hit your desired doneness.
- Pairs well with roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a simple green salad.
