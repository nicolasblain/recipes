## Ingredients

### Burgers
- 400g ground beef (80/20 fat ratio)
- 2 slices cheddar cheese
- 2 thick slices tomato
- 2 thick slices onion
- Mustard and/or mayonnaise
- 4–6 large iceberg lettuce leaves (for wrapping)
- Salt and pepper

### Coleslaw
- 200g green cabbage, thinly shredded
- 1 medium carrot, grated
- 3 tbsp mayonnaise (full-fat)
- 1 tsp apple cider vinegar
- Salt and pepper

## Instructions

1. Make the coleslaw first: combine cabbage and carrot, mix in mayo and vinegar, season, and refrigerate while you cook the burgers.
2. Divide the beef into 2 patties. Season both sides generously with salt and pepper. Don't overwork the meat.
3. Cook in a cast iron pan or on a grill over high heat — 3–4 minutes per side for medium, pressing down slightly once per side. Add cheese in the last minute and cover to melt.
4. Rest the patties for 2 minutes.
5. Lay 2–3 iceberg lettuce leaves flat (overlapping) to form a wrap. Add mustard/mayo, tomato, onion, and the patty.
6. Wrap tightly and eat like a burger. Serve with coleslaw.

## Notes

- Iceberg lettuce works best for wrapping — it's sturdy and adds a satisfying crunch.
- 80/20 ground beef makes a juicier, more flavourful patty than lean beef.
- The coleslaw is made with olive oil mayo, not sweet store-bought dressing, to keep carbs low.
