## Ingredients

- 6 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
- 1 can (400g) whole or crushed tomatoes
- 100g pitted olives (kalamata or green)
- 2 tbsp capers
- 1 medium onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- Salt and pepper
- Fresh parsley to finish (optional)

### To serve
- 2 large handfuls spinach or kale per person, sautéed in olive oil and garlic

## Instructions

1. Pat the chicken thighs dry and season generously with salt and pepper.
2. Heat olive oil in a large, heavy pan or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Place chicken skin-side down and sear without moving for 6–7 minutes until the skin is deep golden and crispy. Flip and sear 3 more minutes. Remove and set aside.
3. In the same pan over medium heat, add the onion and cook for 5 minutes until softened. Add the garlic and oregano, cook for 1 minute.
4. Add the tomatoes, breaking them up with a spoon. Stir in olives and capers. Season with salt and pepper.
5. Nestle the chicken thighs back in, skin-side up, so the skin stays above the sauce.
6. Bring to a simmer, cover, and cook on low heat for 25–30 minutes until chicken is cooked through and falling-off-the-bone tender.
7. Meanwhile, sauté the spinach or kale with olive oil and garlic until wilted.
8. Serve chicken and sauce over the greens. Finish with fresh parsley if desired.

## Notes

- Skin-side up while braising keeps the skin from getting soggy.
- This is better the next day — make a big batch.
- Kalamata olives give a more robust flavour; green olives are milder.
- The sauce is rich enough that no side starch is needed — the greens provide all the bulk.
